Abstract
OrthoAssist: A Scalable Macro-Agent Driven AI Framework for Domain Specific Healthcare Diagnostics is an innovative project designed to revolutionize the way diagnostic tools are developed and deployed within specialized healthcare domains. The framework leverages a macro-agent architecture, enabling the integration of multiple AI models and expert systems to collaboratively analyze medical data, extract relevant insights, and deliver accurate diagnostic recommendations. By focusing on scalability, OrthoAssist ensures adaptability across a range of healthcare disciplines, making it suitable for both large hospital networks and smaller clinical environments.
The system is engineered to support seamless data flow between various modules, facilitating efficient preprocessing, feature extraction, and decision-making processes. OrthoAssist is built with modularity in mind, allowing for easy incorporation of new diagnostic capabilities as medical technologies and datasets evolve. Through its domain-specific approach, the framework aims to address the unique challenges faced by healthcare professionals, such as heterogeneous data sources, complex patient histories, and the need for rapid, reliable diagnostics. Ultimately, OrthoAssist empowers clinicians with advanced AI-driven tools, improving patient outcomes and streamlining clinical workflows.

Chapter 1: Introduction
Background
The healthcare industry faces unprecedented challenges in delivering timely and accurate diagnostics, particularly in specialized domains such as orthopedics. Traditional diagnostic methods often rely heavily on manual interpretation of medical imaging, patient history, and clinical examinations, which can be time-consuming and subject to human error. With the exponential growth of medical data and the increasing complexity of patient cases, there is a critical need for intelligent systems that can augment clinical decision-making. Artificial Intelligence has emerged as a transformative force in healthcare, offering the potential to analyze vast amounts of data, identify patterns, and provide diagnostic recommendations with remarkable accuracy. OrthoAssist addresses this need by proposing a macro-agent driven AI framework specifically designed for domain-specific healthcare diagnostics, combining the strengths of multiple AI agents to deliver comprehensive and reliable diagnostic support.
OrthoAssist addresses this critical need by proposing a macro-agent driven AI framework specifically designed for domain-specific healthcare diagnostics, focusing initially on orthopedics. This innovative framework combines the strengths of multiple specialized AI agents, each designed to handle a particular aspect of the diagnostic process. For instance, one agent might specialize in image analysis (e.g., X-rays, MRIs), another in processing patient history and clinical symptoms, and yet another in integrating laboratory results. By orchestrating these individual agents within a cohesive macro-agent framework, OrthoAssist aims to deliver comprehensive and reliable diagnostic support that surpasses the capabilities of any single AI model or traditional manual approach. This integrated approach ensures a more holistic understanding of the patient's condition, leading to more precise diagnoses and ultimately, improved patient care. The goal is not to replace human clinicians but to empower them with advanced tools that enhance their diagnostic capabilities, reduce their workload, and enable them to focus on complex decision-making and patient interaction.

2.3 Design Details 
The OrthoAssist architecture follows a layered design pattern consisting of five primary layers:
1. Presentation Layer: Web-based interface for healthcare professionals, mobile application for accessibility, dashboard for visualization of diagnostic results and system analytics
2. Application Layer: RESTful API services, macro-agent orchestration engine, session management, and request routing
3. Agent Layer: Central coordinator agent, specialised diagnostic agents (Image Analyser, Pattern Recogniser, Clinical Interpreter, Knowledge Retriever), agent communication protocols, and result aggregation mechanisms
4. Processing Layer: Deep learning inference engines, image preprocessing pipelines, NLP processing for clinical text, data validation and sanitisation, and explainability generation modules
5. Data Layer: Relational database for structured patient data, NoSQL database for unstructured data and logs, secure file storage for medical images, caching layer for frequently accessed data, backup and disaster recovery systems
The system employs microservices architecture principles, enabling independent scaling and deployment of individual components. Communication between agents utilises message queuing systems for asynchronous processing, ensuring system responsiveness and fault tolerance.

The OrthoAssist System follows a modular design strategy to ensure that it remains easy to scale, update, and maintain over time. Each module is built for a specific purpose such as X-ray processing, report generation, or secure storage — and they work together smoothly through well-defined APIs and communication protocols.
3.1 Modules
OrthoAssist is organised into the following core modules:
Frontend ModuleDesigned using Next.js along with Tailwind CSS and Shadcn/UI to provide a fast and smooth user interface for doctors and healthcare professionals.
Backend ModuleBuilt on FastAPI and MCP (Model Context Protocol) architecture, responsible for handling all processing requests and coordinating AI agents.
LLM Agentic ModuleManages the AI-driven reasoning pipeline including triage, medical insights, and report drafting using a Groq-accelerated LLM.
Database & Storage ModuleCombines MongoDB for patient and case data, while Cloudinary securely stores image files and optimised versions of X-rays.
Authentication ModuleClerk handles login, identity verification, and session management to ensure only authorised access to sensitive patient data.
This modular breakdown allows the system to handle complex medical workflows reliably.

Chapter 7: Conclusion
The proposed OrthoAssist – AI-Powered Orthopedic Assistant represents a transformative step in the integration of artificial intelligence, medical imaging, and digital healthcare systems. Designed as a modular and scalable architecture using FastAPI and Next.js, the system combines deep learning, large language models (LLMs), and agent-based coordination to provide a complete orthopedic diagnostic pipeline — from X-ray analysis to report generation.
Through the implementation of YOLO-based object detection, OrthoAssist is capable of identifying fractures in musculoskeletal X-rays with high precision and speed. The use of specialized hand and leg detection agents, coupled with a body part classification mechanism, ensures optimal model routing for accurate anatomical analysis. Additionally, the triage agent dynamically assesses injury severity using clinical rules and AI scoring, providing clear Red/Amber/Green classifications that assist in rapid decision-making for doctors and health professionals.
The LLM-based report generation module, powered by Groq acceleration, converts complex medical data into concise, human-readable summaries and recommendations. This enables clinicians, paramedics, and patients to understand diagnostic outcomes without relying on specialized radiological expertise. The integration with Cloudinary and MongoDB ensures secure, scalable storage of patient data, medical images, and generated reports.
Furthermore, OrthoAssist’s agentic MCP framework facilitates asynchronous processing, inter-agent communication, and model context sharing, making the system highly extensible for future medical applications — including orthopedic anomaly segmentation, comparative bone density analysis, and surgical planning assistance. Its real-time feedback, automated analytics dashboard, and intelligent report history tracking make it a powerful tool for both individual practitioners and institutional healthcare systems.
The project demonstrates how AI and multimodal intelligence can significantly enhance orthopedic diagnostics by reducing interpretation delays from hours to minutes. By democratizing expert-level analysis through an accessible digital interface, OrthoAssist bridges the technological divide between urban healthcare centers and rural clinics, empowering medical staff in low-resource settings.
In conclusion, OrthoAssist stands as a proof-of-concept for next-generation orthopedic AI systems that unite efficiency, interpretability, and clinical utility. It has the potential to redefine orthopedic diagnostics by offering fast, explainable, and patient-friendly medical insights while maintaining scalability, modularity, and compliance with healthcare data standards.
Future developments may include expanding the system’s dataset diversity, integrating multimodal data (CT, MRI), incorporating federated learning for privacy-preserving AI training, and introducing voice-assisted diagnostic explanations for enhanced accessibility.
