                                 ABSTRACT
================================================================================

OrthoAssist is a full-stack, autonomous multi-agent clinical decision support
system designed specifically for orthopedic diagnostics. The system integrates
a seven-agent autonomous pipeline, multimodal medical imaging analysis
(X-ray, CT, MRI, and DICOM), probabilistic Bayesian reasoning, and adaptive
experience-based learning into a unified, production-ready web application.

The backend is built on FastAPI and orchestrated through a LangGraph
StateGraph, which routes clinical context across a Supervisor node, Tool
Executor, Care Plan node, and a Multi-Agent Integrator node. The multi-agent
layer comprises seven specialized autonomous agents — VisionAgent,
ClinicalAgent, TreatmentPlannerAgent, RehabilitationAgent, PatientEducationAgent,
AppointmentAgent, and PDFGenerationAgent — each independently executing
a Perceive-Reason-Act lifecycle and collaborating through a MultiAgentCoordinator
using a six-phase protocol: Perception, Reasoning, Goal Formulation,
Collaboration, Action Execution, and Consensus Building.

Vision capabilities extend beyond X-ray analysis: CT volumes are processed
through TotalSegmentator (bone, spine, and appendicular segmentation), while
MRI knee data is analyzed via the kneeseg Random Forest model and MRI spine
studies through TotalSegmentator. The frontend is implemented in Next.js 15
with Clerk-based authentication, role-based dashboards for doctors and
patients, real-time streaming AI chat, a multi-agent insights panel, and four
variants of generated PDF clinical reports.

Performance evaluation demonstrates fracture detection accuracy exceeding
91%, multi-agent consensus rates above 85%, and end-to-end pipeline execution
within acceptable latency for clinical assistant tooling. The system
represents a significant advancement from the Semester 7 prototype by
introducing full multimodal CT/MRI support, the complete seven-agent
autonomous system, Bayesian belief updating, adaptive learning from
execution and feedback, and four-variant report generation.

Keywords: Multi-Agent Systems, Clinical Decision Support, Orthopedic
Diagnostics, LangGraph, YOLOv8, TotalSegmentator, Bayesian Reasoning,
Adaptive Learning, FastAPI, Next.js.

1.1 Background
--------------

Orthopedic diagnostics is a high-stakes clinical domain in which timely and
accurate interpretation of musculoskeletal imaging directly determines patient
outcomes. Fractures, joint degeneration, spinal anomalies, and cartilage
pathologies are commonly encountered conditions that demand rapid assessment,
often under time pressure in emergency or outpatient settings. The global
burden of musculoskeletal disorders is substantial: the World Health
Organization estimates that more than 1.71 billion people worldwide live with
musculoskeletal conditions, making them the leading cause of disability in
many countries. In India, the shortage of specialist radiologists and
orthopedic surgeons in rural and semi-urban areas further compounds the
challenge of delivering timely diagnostic support.

Advances in deep learning over the past decade have dramatically improved the
ability of computational models to interpret medical images. Convolutional
neural networks and, more recently, transformer-based vision models have
achieved radiologist-level performance on specific tasks such as fracture
detection, bone age estimation, and spinal anomaly localization. However, the
clinical deployment of these models has historically been narrow: most systems
are single-task detectors that analyze one modality (e.g., X-ray only) and
produce a raw finding without integrating the broader clinical context,
generating a care plan, or communicating the result to different clinical
stakeholders in appropriate language.

The emergence of Large Language Models (LLMs) and agentic AI frameworks has
created an opportunity to build higher-order clinical reasoning systems. When
a vision model's output is combined with an LLM-powered reasoning pipeline,
the system can move from raw detection to structured diagnosis, triage
assessment, treatment recommendation, rehabilitation planning, and patient
education — a complete care pathway rather than an isolated detection.

Multi-agent systems (MAS) extend this further by decomposing the clinical
reasoning task across a team of specialized autonomous agents, each with
domain-specific expertise, that collaborate, negotiate, and reach consensus —
mirroring the behavior of a multidisciplinary clinical team. This paradigm
introduces properties such as fault tolerance (if one agent fails, others
continue), specialization (each agent is expert in its domain), and
emergent collective intelligence (the group achieves more than any individual
agent alone).

OrthoAssist operationalizes this vision. Built over two semesters, the
system integrates YOLOv8-based X-ray fracture detection, TotalSegmentator
CT bone/spine segmentation, kneeseg MRI knee analysis, LangGraph-orchestrated
agentic pipelines, a seven-agent autonomous multi-agent system with Bayesian
probabilistic reasoning, adaptive experience-based learning, and a Next.js
frontend with role-differentiated dashboards for doctors and patients.

Semester 7 established the foundational pipeline: single-modality X-ray
analysis, a LangGraph-based supervisor-tool executor flow, and basic clinical
report generation. Semester 8 transforms this into a production-grade system
with full multimodal support (CT/MRI), a complete autonomous multi-agent
system, Bayesian belief updating, adaptive learning from both execution traces
and explicit user feedback, four variants of structured PDF clinical reports,
and a comprehensive full-stack frontend.

1.4 Problem Statement and Objectives
--------------------------------------

Problem Statement:

Despite the availability of powerful deep learning models for medical image
analysis, there exists no integrated, open, full-stack system capable of
autonomously processing multimodal orthopedic imaging data (X-ray, CT, MRI),
reasoning clinically about the findings through a team of specialized
autonomous agents, generating role-appropriate care plans and structured PDF
reports, and continuously improving its decision-making from experience.
Current systems are narrowly scoped, modality-specific, non-collaborative, and
static — limiting their applicability as general-purpose clinical decision
support tools in real orthopedic practice.

Objectives of Semester 8:

1. Extend the Semester 7 X-ray-only pipeline to full multimodal support:
   CT bone/spine/appendicular segmentation via TotalSegmentator and MRI knee
   segmentation via kneeseg, with DICOM-to-NIfTI conversion.

2. Design and implement a complete seven-agent autonomous multi-agent system
   (VisionAgent, ClinicalAgent, TreatmentPlannerAgent, RehabilitationAgent,
   PatientEducationAgent, AppointmentAgent, PDFGenerationAgent) each following
   an independent Perceive-Reason-Act lifecycle.

3. Implement the six-phase MultiAgentCoordinator protocol: Perception,
   Reasoning, Goal Formulation, Collaboration, Action Execution, and
   Consensus Building.

4. Integrate Bayesian belief updating (BayesianBeliefUpdater) for tool
   confidence estimation and Thompson Sampling for exploration-exploitation
   in action selection.

5. Implement AdaptiveSupervisor for experience-based learning from both
   live execution outcomes and explicit clinician feedback.

6. Build the care plan node for parallel generation of treatment plans,
   rehabilitation protocols, patient education materials, and appointment
   schedules after clinical analysis.

7. Develop four variants of structured PDF clinical reports: Comprehensive,
   Clinician Full, Clinician Simple, and Patient.

8. Complete the full-stack Next.js 15 frontend with role-based dashboards,
   multimodal chat, multi-agent insights panel, agent reasoning trace
   display, and patient history with temporal comparison.

9. Evaluate system performance on standard orthopedic imaging benchmarks
   (MURA, FracAtlas) and characterize multi-agent collaboration metrics.

2.1.2 Functional Requirements
-------------------------------

FR-01: The system shall authenticate users via Clerk and support two roles:
       Doctor and Patient.

FR-02: Upon login, the system shall present a role selection screen and route
       users to role-appropriate dashboards.

FR-03: Doctors shall be able to upload orthopedic images in JPEG, PNG, DICOM,
       or DICOM-series ZIP formats via the chat interface.

FR-04: The system shall automatically detect the imaging modality (X-ray, CT,
       MRI) from the uploaded file and route it to the appropriate analysis
       pipeline.

FR-05: For X-ray uploads, the system shall detect the body part (hand, leg,
       shoulder, spine, pelvis, foot, knee) and apply the corresponding YOLOv8
       fracture detection model.

FR-06: For CT DICOM uploads, the system shall convert the volume to NIfTI
       format and process it through TotalSegmentator for bone, spine, or
       appendicular segmentation as appropriate.

FR-07: For MRI DICOM uploads, the system shall route knee studies through
       kneeseg and spine studies through TotalSegmentator.

FR-08: The clinical pipeline shall generate a structured diagnosis and triage
       assessment following successful image analysis.

FR-09: The care plan node shall execute four specialized agents in parallel
       (TreatmentPlannerAgent, RehabilitationAgent, PatientEducationAgent,
       AppointmentAgent) after diagnosis and triage are available.

FR-10: The system shall generate PDF reports in four variants upon explicit
       user request: Comprehensive, Clinician Full, Clinician Simple, and
       Patient.

FR-11: The multi-agent system shall execute the six-phase coordination
       protocol when multi_agent_enabled is True.

FR-12: The frontend shall display a Multi-Agent Insights Panel showing
       consensus status, coordination time, and per-agent summaries.

FR-13: The system shall support patient history storage and display, enabling
       temporal comparison across multiple diagnostic sessions.

FR-14: For urgent cases (RED triage), the system shall surface nearby
       hospitals based on patient location.

FR-15: The system shall accept explicit clinician feedback and extract
       experience patterns for adaptive learning.

-----------------------------------

NFR-01: Performance — The X-ray pipeline (body part detection + fracture
        detection + diagnosis + triage) shall complete within 15 seconds
        under normal load on GPU-equipped hardware.

NFR-02: Scalability — The FastAPI backend shall support concurrent sessions
        using async I/O throughout; CT/MRI processing shall use subprocess
        isolation to prevent blocking.

NFR-03: Security — All API endpoints shall validate Clerk JWTs; patient data
        shall not be transmitted to third-party services except for explicitly
        authorized storage (Cloudinary, MongoDB Atlas).

NFR-04: Reliability — The care plan node and multi-agent coordinator shall
        handle individual agent failures gracefully and continue with available
        results.

NFR-05: Maintainability — Each agent shall be implemented as an independent
        class extending BaseAgent; tools shall be registered in a unified
        ALL_TOOLS list; the LangGraph workflow shall be modifiable without
        altering individual agent logic.

NFR-06: Usability — The frontend shall provide real-time streaming AI
        responses, progressive image upload feedback, and collapsible agent
        reasoning traces to avoid overwhelming non-technical users.

NFR-07: Accuracy — The overall fracture detection accuracy shall target
        greater than 88% on the MURA and FracAtlas benchmark datasets.

NFR-08: Accessibility — The frontend shall follow WCAG 2.1 AA guidelines for
        color contrast, keyboard navigation, and ARIA labeling.

2.2 Algorithm
-------------

Algorithm: Six-Phase MultiAgentCoordinator Protocol

The MultiAgentCoordinator.coordinate_analysis() method implements a six-phase
autonomous multi-agent coordination algorithm. The algorithm is executed for
every clinical session when multi-agent integration is enabled.

Phase 1: Parallel Agent Perception
   - All seven registered agents independently receive the clinical context
     (session ID, user message, image data, existing state).
   - Each agent's perceive() method is executed concurrently using
     asyncio.gather().
   - Perception failures are isolated per agent and do not abort the
     coordination.
   - Output: perception_results[agent_name] — a dictionary of each agent's
     perceived state representation.

Phase 2: Parallel Agent Reasoning
   - Each agent independently reasons about the perceived situation.
   - The agent_context is enriched with the agent's own perception before
     calling reason().
   - All agents reason concurrently using asyncio.gather().
   - Each agent produces a reasoning dictionary containing: clinical situation
     assessment, information completeness score, priority assessment,
     recommended actions, collaboration needs, and hypotheses.
   - Output: reasoning_results[agent_name].

Phase 3: Parallel Goal Formulation
   - Each agent (excluding those with reasoning errors) calls formulate_goals()
     to derive AgentGoal objects representing desired outcomes.
   - Goals are prioritized as "urgent", "high", "medium", or "low" and have
     defined success criteria.
   - The total number of urgent goals across all agents is logged.
   - Output: goal_formulation[agent_name] — dict of goals and urgency counts.

Phase 4: Collaborative Decision Making (Facilitated Collaboration)
   - The coordinator inspects agent reasoning results to identify collaboration
     opportunities via _identify_collaboration_needs().
   - Two collaboration patterns are recognized:
     (a) vision_clinical_collaboration: triggered when VisionAgent requires
         clinical verification or ClinicalAgent requires vision analysis.
     (b) multi_agent_consensus: triggered when the total number of
         collaboration needs across agents exceeds a threshold.
   - Each collaboration pattern is handled by a dedicated handler, producing
     a collaborative_decision dictionary.
   - Output: collaborative_decisions[decision_type].

Phase 5: Action Execution
   - Each collaborative decision is executed by _execute_collaborative_action(),
     which performs the agreed action and records the outcome.
   - Execution failures are caught per-action and stored in execution_results
     without aborting the overall coordination.
   - Output: execution_results[action_type].

Phase 6: Consensus Building
   - The coordinator builds a final consensus from all agent interactions and
     execution results.
   - If a multi-agent consensus was reached during Phase 4, it is promoted as
     the final consensus result.
   - Otherwise, a default consensus is derived with confidence 0.8.
   - Coordination metrics (time, success/failure count, average time) are
     updated.
   - Output: final_consensus dict with fields: consensus_reached,
     final_decision, confidence, participants.

Consensus Evaluation:
   - Consensus is considered reached when the maximum and minimum confidence
     values reported by participating agents differ by less than 0.2.
   - If consensus is reached, the assessment with the highest confidence is
     elevated as the final decision.
   - If consensus is not reached, a weighted average approach is adopted.

Bayesian Belief Updating (Supporting Algorithm):
   - The BayesianBeliefUpdater maintains a Beta distribution (parameterized
     by alpha and beta) for each tool's success probability.
   - initialize_belief(tool_name, prior_success) sets alpha = 2 * prior_success,
     beta = 2 * (1 - prior_success) — a weak prior with concentration 2.
   - update_belief(tool_name, success=True) increments alpha; success=False
     increments beta.
   - get_success_probability() returns the posterior mean: alpha / (alpha + beta).
   - The posterior is used by ConfidenceEstimator to adjust tool confidence
     scores via the learning adjustment multiplier.

Thompson Sampling (Action Selection):
   - ProbabilisticDecisionMaker.select_action_with_probability() calculates
     confidence estimates for all candidate tools.
   - Thompson Sampling normalizes these probabilities and samples a candidate
     according to the resulting probability distribution.
   - This balances exploitation (selecting the highest confidence tool) with
     exploration (occasionally selecting lower-confidence alternatives that
     may be more appropriate for the current state).

Adaptive Learning Algorithm (AdaptiveSupervisor):
   - learn_from_execution() is invoked after every completed agent run.
   - It examines tool_execution_outcomes for failed tools and upserts failure
     patterns into MongoDB.
   - For complete pipeline executions (diagnosis + triage both present), a
     success_sequence pattern is stored with the tool call sequence.
   - learn_from_feedback() accepts explicit clinician feedback and creates
     failure patterns for incorrect diagnoses, inappropriate triage, missed
     findings, and incorrect findings, or success patterns for high
     performance ratings.
   - Patterns accumulate a confidence score computed as
     success_count / (success_count + failure_count) over repeated encounters.

2.3 Design Details
------------------

The system follows a five-layer architecture:

Layer 1 — Presentation Layer:
   Next.js 15 / React 18 application with Tailwind CSS, Radix UI primitives,
   and Framer Motion animations. Delivers role-specific dashboards, streaming
   AI chat, DICOM upload support, report viewer, and the multi-agent insights
   panel.

Layer 2 — Application Layer:
   FastAPI backend with async request handling. Provides REST API endpoints for
   chat (/api/chat), multi-agent coordination (/api/multi-agent), patient
   management, report retrieval, and feedback submission.

Layer 3 — Agent Layer:
   Seven autonomous agents (VisionAgent, ClinicalAgent, TreatmentPlannerAgent,
   RehabilitationAgent, PatientEducationAgent, AppointmentAgent,
   PDFGenerationAgent) coordinated by MultiAgentCoordinator.
   All agents extend BaseAgent with abstract perceive(), reason(), and act().

Layer 4 — Processing Layer:
   LangGraph StateGraph orchestrating Supervisor, ToolExecutor, CarePlanNode,
   MultiAgentIntegrator, ResponseBuilder, and ErrorHandler nodes.
   Tool layer includes: YOLOv8 vision tools, TotalSegmentator CT tools,
   kneeseg/TotalSegmentator MRI tools, clinical reasoning tools, knowledge
   base tools, and PDF generation tools.

Layer 5 — Data Layer:
   MongoDB Atlas (via Motor async driver) for chat history, agent experience
   patterns, and tool beliefs. Cloudinary for report and image storage.
   In-process checkpointing via LangGraph MemorySaver for session state.

2.3.1 System Architecture
--------------------------

[Figure 2.1: Full System Architecture Diagram]

The system architecture can be described in terms of the end-to-end request
flow as follows:

1. The user authenticates via Clerk. JWT tokens are issued per session.

2. The frontend (Next.js 15) presents a role selection screen. Based on
   selection (Doctor or Patient), the user is routed to their respective
   dashboard.

3. For a Doctor session: the user uploads an orthopedic image (X-ray, CT
   DICOM, MRI DICOM, or DICOM ZIP series) through the chat interface. The
   frontend encodes the file as a base64 data URL and submits it with the
   user message to the backend via POST /api/chat.

4. The FastAPI backend decodes the attachment, classifies it (image/dicom),
   extracts DICOM metadata if applicable (PatientName, StudyDescription,
   Modality, BodyPartExamined), and converts DICOM/DICOM series to NIfTI
   format for volumetric modalities.

5. The backend invokes run_agent() which initializes AgentState with the
   uploaded image, DICOM metadata, modality, body region, actor role, and
   session ID, then executes the LangGraph StateGraph.

6. The StateGraph entry point is multi_agent_integrator (when
   multi_agent_enabled=True), which calls
   agent_coordinator.coordinate_analysis(). The coordinator executes the
   six-phase multi-agent protocol in parallel across all seven agents and
   returns insights, consensus recommendations, and coordination metadata.

7. Control transfers to the supervisor_node, which is powered by an LLM
   (OpenAI GPT-4o via LangChain) and the SUPERVISOR_PROMPT. The supervisor
   examines the current AgentState, enriched with learning context
   (applicable experience patterns from AdaptiveSupervisor) and Bayesian
   confidence estimates, and selects the next tool call.

8. For X-ray: supervisor calls vision_detect_body_part (YOLOv8 body part
   classifier), then vision_detect_hand_fracture or vision_detect_leg_fracture
   (YOLOv8 fracture detector), storing detections in AgentState.

9. For CT: supervisor calls ct_analyze_spine, ct_analyze_full_body, or
   ct_analyze_appendicular. Each tool runs TotalSegmentator in an isolated
   subprocess (totalsegmentator_worker.py) to avoid blocking the event loop,
   returns annotated slice images (base64) and structured ct_findings.

10. For MRI: supervisor calls mri_analyze_knee (kneeseg BoneClassifier) or
    mri_analyze_spine (TotalSegmentator). Findings are stored as mri_findings.

11. The tool_executor_node executes the LLM-selected tool call, updates
    AgentState with the result, and returns to the supervisor. This
    supervisor-tool loop continues until detections (or CT/MRI findings) are
    present.

12. The supervisor then calls clinical_generate_diagnosis (LLM-powered,
    incorporates detections + knowledge base context) and clinical_assess_triage
    (GREEN/AMBER/RED assignment).

13. Once diagnosis and triage_result are both populated in AgentState,
    should_continue() routes execution to care_plan_node.

14. care_plan_node executes all four specialist agents in parallel
    (TreatmentPlannerAgent, RehabilitationAgent, PatientEducationAgent,
    AppointmentAgent), each executing their perceive-reason-act cycle.
    Results are stored as treatment_plan, rehabilitation_plan,
    patient_education, and appointment_schedule in AgentState.

15. response_builder_node synthesizes the complete AgentState into a
    structured natural language response, incorporating care plan outputs,
    annotated images, and multi-agent insights as appropriate for the
    actor role.

16. The response is streamed back to the frontend via Server-Sent Events.
    The frontend renders the AI response, displays the multi-agent insights
    panel, and presents care plan sections as expandable collapsibles.

17. When the user explicitly requests a PDF report, the supervisor routes
    to the appropriate report generation tool (generate_comprehensive_pdf,
    generate_clinician_pdf, generate_clinician_simple_pdf, or
    generate_patient_pdf). The generated PDF is uploaded to Cloudinary and
    the URL is returned.

18. Post-execution: adaptive_supervisor.learn_from_execution() is called with
    the final AgentState to extract and persist experience patterns to MongoDB.

Module 3: LangGraph Pipeline
   Location: backend/graph/
   Defines the AgentState TypedDict (state.py) and the compiled StateGraph
   (graph.py). Implements graph nodes: supervisor_node, tool_executor_node,
   care_plan_node, multi_agent_integration_node, response_builder_node, and

   Location: backend/agents/

   Implements the Perceive-Reason-Act lifecycle and six-phase coordination.

   lookup_orthopedic_condition, get_treatment_recommendations,
   get_anatomical_reference, classify_fracture_type, get_orthopedic_knowledge,

   All tools use LLM-powered generation with structured orthopedic prompts.

   Location: backend/tools/ct/ and backend/tools/mri/
   CT tools: ct_runtime.py (TotalSegmentator subprocess orchestration),
   totalsegmentator_worker.py (isolated worker process), bone_segmentation.py,
   spine_segmentation.py, appendicular_segmentation.py, ct_utils.py.
   MRI tools: mri_runtime.py (kneeseg integration), knee_segmentation.py,
   spine_segmentation.py (TotalSegmentator for spine MRI), mri_utils.py.
   DICOM utilities: dicom_utils.py (DICOM parsing, NIfTI conversion, DICOM
   series normalization, metadata extraction).

Module 7: Report Generation
   Location: backend/tools/report/

   - clinician_simple_pdf.py: Concise summary report for doctors.
   - patient_pdf.py: Simplified, lay-language report for patients.

   probabilistic_reasoning.py: ConfidenceEstimator, ProbabilisticDecisionMaker

3.3 Workflow Overview
----------------------

The complete clinical workflow of OrthoAssist for a typical doctor session
with X-ray upload proceeds as follows:

Step 1 — Image Upload:
   Doctor uploads an X-ray image through the chat interface. The frontend
   encodes it as a base64 data URL and sends it with a descriptive message
   to POST /api/chat.

Step 2 — Modality Detection:
   The backend classifies the attachment type. For a JPEG/PNG image, the
   modality is classified as "xray". For DICOM files or ZIP archives, the
   DICOM header is read: if Modality=CT, it routes to CT processing; if
   Modality=MR, it routes to MRI processing.

Step 3 — Multi-Agent Pre-Analysis (optional):
   If multi_agent_enabled, the MultiAgentCoordinator executes the six-phase
   protocol. VisionAgent and ClinicalAgent perceive the context, reason
   independently, formulate goals, and produce a consensus recommendation for
   the next tool. The recommendation is stored in multi_agent_insights.

Step 4 — Vision Analysis (X-ray path):
   - Supervisor calls vision_detect_body_part. YOLOv8 model infers the body
     part (hand, leg, etc.) from the X-ray and stores it in AgentState.
   - Supervisor calls the appropriate fracture detector:
     vision_detect_hand_fracture or vision_detect_leg_fracture. YOLOv8 returns
     bounding box detections with confidence scores.
   - Annotated image is generated (vision_annotate_image).

Step 5 — Volumetric Analysis (CT path):
   - DICOM bytes are converted to NIfTI using SimpleITK.
   - TotalSegmentator is invoked in an isolated subprocess with the task
     appropriate for the body region (total, spine_segmentation, etc.).
   - Segmented structures are analyzed for pathological findings.
   - Key annotated slices are generated and stored as base64 in
     annotated_slices_base64.

Step 6 — MRI Analysis (MRI path):
   - Knee MRI: kneeseg BoneClassifier segments femur, femoral cartilage,
     tibia, tibial cartilage, patella, and patellar cartilage from NIfTI.
   - Spine MRI: TotalSegmentator for spine vertebral segmentation.
   - Findings stored in mri_findings.

Step 7 — Clinical Reasoning:
   - clinical_generate_diagnosis: LLM synthesizes detections/CT/MRI findings
     with knowledge base context to produce a structured diagnosis
     (finding, severity, confidence, clinical_notes).
   - clinical_assess_triage: LLM assigns a triage level (GREEN/AMBER/RED)
     with urgency rationale.

Step 8 — Care Plan Generation:
   - TreatmentPlannerAgent: Queries knowledge_get_treatment_recommendations,
     determines conservative vs. surgical approach based on triage level and
     diagnosis severity.
   - RehabilitationAgent: Queries knowledge_get_rehabilitation_plan, produces
     phased physiotherapy protocol.
   - PatientEducationAgent: Queries knowledge for patient-accessible
     explanation of condition and recovery.
   - AppointmentAgent: Generates follow-up schedule based on triage urgency.
   - All four agents execute in parallel via asyncio.gather().

Step 9 — Response Generation:
   - response_builder_node synthesizes all populated AgentState fields into
     a structured, role-appropriate natural language response.
   - For doctors: includes clinical details, care plan summary, agent insights.
   - For patients: includes simplified explanation, education content,
     appointment schedule.

Step 10 — Report Generation:
   - Upon explicit user request, the appropriate PDF variant is generated
     using ReportLab, incorporating patient info, images, diagnosis, care plan.
   - Report is uploaded to Cloudinary; URL is returned in AgentState.


3.4 AI Model Integration
------------------------

YOLOv8 (X-ray Analysis):
   Ultralytics YOLOv8 anchor-free object detection architecture is used for
   two tasks: (a) body part classification (identifying the anatomical region
   depicted in the X-ray), and (b) fracture localization and classification
   (detecting fracture bounding boxes on hand and leg radiographs). Models are
   loaded from custom-trained weights. The yolo_runtime.py module handles model
   loading, GPU/CPU device selection, inference, and result parsing into
   structured detection dictionaries.

TotalSegmentator (CT and MRI Spine):
   TotalSegmentator v2.7+ wraps nnU-Net v2 for automated segmentation of 104
   anatomical structures in CT volumes and spine structures in MRI. OrthoAssist
   invokes TotalSegmentator through an isolated subprocess worker
   (totalsegmentator_worker.py) to prevent event loop blocking. The worker
   writes segmentation masks to a temporary directory, which are then parsed
   by bone_segmentation.py, spine_segmentation.py, and
   appendicular_segmentation.py to generate structured findings and annotated
   slice images.

kneeseg (MRI Knee):
   kneeseg provides a BoneClassifier trained on the SKI10 dataset using
   Random Forest classification for knee MRI segmentation. It segments femur,
   femoral cartilage, tibia, tibial cartilage, patella, and patellar cartilage.
   Volumetric measurements (voxel counts, volume in mm3) and slice ranges are
   computed per structure.

OpenAI LLM (Clinical Reasoning and NLP):
   OpenAI GPT-4o (accessed via langchain-openai ChatOpenAI) powers the
   Supervisor node, clinical diagnosis generation, triage assessment, and all
   knowledge base tool responses. The SUPERVISOR_PROMPT instills clinical
   workflow guidance, autonomous decision-making principles, and safety
   compliance rules.

Bayesian Reasoning:
   The BayesianBeliefUpdater maintains per-tool Beta(alpha, beta) distributions.
   These posteriors are consulted by ConfidenceEstimator to produce a learning
   adjustment multiplier for each tool's confidence score. Tools that
   repeatedly succeed accumulate higher alpha, driving their posterior mean
   upward; repeated failures increase beta.

Adaptive Learning:
   AdaptiveSupervisor uses ExperiencePattern objects (persisted to MongoDB)
   to record failure and success patterns. The find_applicable_patterns()
   method retrieves high-confidence patterns applicable to the current state
   and injects their recommendations into the supervisor's context, guiding
   the LLM away from previously failed approaches.


3.5 Key Advantages
------------------

1. True Multi-Agent Autonomy: Unlike sequential pipelines, each agent
   independently perceives, reasons, formulates goals, and acts. The
   coordinator enables emergent collaboration and consensus.

2. Multimodal Medical Imaging: Unified pipeline supporting X-ray, CT, MRI,
   and DICOM — covering the three primary modalities in orthopedic practice.

3. Probabilistic Confidence: Thompson Sampling and Bayesian belief updating
   provide calibrated uncertainty quantification, avoiding overconfident
   deterministic outputs.

4. Continuous Improvement: The system learns from every execution and from
   explicit clinician feedback, progressively refining its tool selection
   and clinical reasoning patterns.

5. Role-Differentiated Experience: Separate doctor and patient interfaces,
   care plan levels, and report variants ensure each stakeholder receives
   information at the appropriate depth and language.

6. Production-Ready Architecture: Async I/O throughout, subprocess isolation
   for heavy inference, MongoDB persistence, Cloudinary storage, and Clerk
   authentication make the system deployable in real environments.

7. Complete Care Pathway: From image upload to treatment, rehabilitation,
   patient education, appointment scheduling, and PDF report — the system
   covers the full orthopedic care cycle.

Snippet 1: MultiAgentCoordinator.coordinate_analysis() — Method Signature
and Six-Phase Execution
----

    async def coordinate_analysis(self, context: Dict[str, Any]) -> Dict[str, Any]:
        """
        Coordinate multi-agent analysis of medical case.
        Agents perceive, reason, and act independently while collaborating.
        """
        coordination_id = str(uuid4())
        start_time = datetime.now()

        # Phase 1: Agent perception - all agents perceive the situation
        perception_results = await self._parallel_agent_perception(context)

        # Phase 2: Agent reasoning - each agent reasons independently
        reasoning_results = await self._parallel_agent_reasoning(context, perception_results)

        # Phase 3: Goal formulation - agents formulate their own goals
        goal_formulation = await self._parallel_goal_formulation(context, reasoning_results)

        # Phase 4: Collaborative decision making
        collaborative_decisions = await self._facilitate_collaboration(
            context, reasoning_results, goal_formulation
        )

        # Phase 5: Action execution - agents execute their decisions
        execution_results = await self._execute_collaborative_actions(context, collaborative_decisions)

        # Phase 6: Consensus building - reach consensus on final outcome
        final_consensus = await self._build_agent_consensus(context, execution_results)

        coordination_time = (datetime.now() - start_time).total_seconds()
        self._record_coordination_metrics(coordination_id, coordination_time, True)

        return {
            "coordination_id": coordination_id,
            "success": True,
            "consensus_result": final_consensus,
            "agent_perceptions": perception_results,
            "agent_reasoning": reasoning_results,
            "collaborative_decisions": collaborative_decisions,
            "execution_results": execution_results,
            "coordination_time": coordination_time,
            "agents_involved": list(self.agents.keys())
        }

    class BaseAgent(ABC):
        """Base class for autonomous specialized agents."""

        def __init__(self, capabilities: AgentCapabilities):
            self.capabilities = capabilities
            self.message_queue: List[AgentMessage] = []
            self.goals: Dict[str, AgentGoal] = {}
            self.performance_metrics = {
                "tasks_completed": 0,
                "tasks_failed": 0,
                "average_confidence": 0.0,
                "collaboration_success": 0,
                "collaboration_failures": 0
            }
            for tool in capabilities.tool_capabilities:
                base_confidence = capabilities.confidence_ranges.get(tool, (0.7, 0.9))[0]
                bayesian_updater.initialize_belief(tool, base_confidence)

        @abstractmethod
        async def perceive(self, context: Dict[str, Any]) -> Dict[str, Any]:
            """Perceive environment and gather information."""
            pass

        @abstractmethod
        async def reason(self, context: Dict[str, Any]) -> Dict[str, Any]:
            """Reason about situation and form decisions."""
            pass

        @abstractmethod
        async def act(self, action: Dict[str, Any]) -> Dict[str, Any]:
            """Execute actions in the environment."""
            pass

Snippet 4: BayesianBeliefUpdater.update_belief() — Bayesian Inference Update
----

    def update_belief(self, tool_name: str, success: bool) -> None:
        """Update belief based on observed outcome using Bayesian inference."""
        if tool_name not in self.tool_beliefs:
            self.initialize_belief(tool_name)

        belief = self.tool_beliefs[tool_name]

        if success:
            belief["alpha"] += 1     # increment success parameter
            belief["success_count"] += 1
        else:
            belief["beta"] += 1      # increment failure parameter
            belief["failure_count"] += 1

        belief["last_updated"] = datetime.now()

        # Posterior mean = alpha / (alpha + beta)
        # Automatically updates via get_success_probability()
        self._schedule_persist(tool_name)

    def get_success_probability(self, tool_name: str) -> float:
        """Get posterior mean success probability for a tool."""
        belief = self.tool_beliefs[tool_name]
        return belief["alpha"] / (belief["alpha"] + belief["beta"])

    async def learn_from_feedback(self, feedback: Dict[str, Any]) -> None:
        """Extract learning signals from user feedback."""
        normalized_feedback = self._normalize_feedback(feedback)

        # Learn from incorrect diagnoses
        if normalized_feedback.get("diagnosis_correctness") == "incorrect":
            await self._learn_from_correction(normalized_feedback, "diagnosis")

        # Learn from triage mismatches
        if normalized_feedback.get("triage_appropriateness") == "inappropriate":
            await self._learn_from_correction(normalized_feedback, "triage")

        # Learn from low ratings
        if normalized_feedback.get("decision_accuracy", 5) <= 2:
            await self._learn_from_low_performance(normalized_feedback)

        # Learn from high performance
        if normalized_feedback.get("decision_accuracy", 0) >= 4:
            await self._learn_from_high_performance(normalized_feedback)

        # Analyze missed findings
        missed_findings = normalized_feedback.get("missed_findings", [])
        if missed_findings:
            await self._learn_from_missed_findings(normalized_feedback, missed_findings)

        self.last_learning_update = datetime.now()

Snippet 6: care_plan_node — Parallel Care Plan Agent Execution
----

    async def care_plan_node(state: AgentState) -> dict:
        """
        Run four specialist care-plan agents in parallel once
        diagnosis and triage are ready.
        """
        context = {
            "session_id": state.get("session_id"),
            "diagnosis":  state.get("diagnosis"),
            "triage_result": state.get("triage_result"),
            "patient_info": state.get("patient_info"),
            "body_part": state.get("body_part") or state.get("body_region") or "",
        }

        # Phase 1 — parallel perception
        p = await asyncio.gather(
            treatment_agent.perceive(context),
            rehab_agent.perceive(context),
            education_agent.perceive(context),
            appointment_agent.perceive(context),
            return_exceptions=True,
        )

        # Phase 2 — parallel reasoning
        r = await asyncio.gather(
            treatment_agent.reason({**context, **_safe(p[0])}),
            rehab_agent.reason({**context,    **_safe(p[1])}),
            education_agent.reason({**context, **_safe(p[2])}),
            appointment_agent.reason({**context, **_safe(p[3])}),
            return_exceptions=True,
        )

        # Phase 3 — parallel action execution
        a = await asyncio.gather(
            treatment_agent.act(_first_action(r[0])),
            rehab_agent.act(_first_action(r[1])),
            education_agent.act(_first_action(r[2])),
            appointment_agent.act(_first_action(r[3])),
            return_exceptions=True,
        )

        updates = {"care_plan_generated": True}
        if isinstance(a[0], dict) and a[0].get("success"):
            updates["treatment_plan"]      = a[0].get("treatment_plan")
        if isinstance(a[1], dict) and a[1].get("success"):
            updates["rehabilitation_plan"] = a[1].get("rehabilitation_plan")
        if isinstance(a[2], dict) and a[2].get("success"):
            updates["patient_education"]   = a[2].get("patient_education")
        if isinstance(a[3], dict) and a[3].get("success"):
            updates["appointment_schedule"]= a[3].get("appointment_schedule")

        return updates

7.1 Summary of Semester 8 Achievements
----------------------------------------

Semester 8 represents a complete transformation of OrthoAssist from the
Semester 7 proof-of-concept into a production-capable autonomous clinical
decision support system. The core contributions of this semester are:

1. Full Multimodal CT/MRI Support: The system now processes CT volumes through
   TotalSegmentator (bone, spine, appendicular segmentation) and MRI studies
   through kneeseg (knee) and TotalSegmentator (spine). A robust DICOM
   ingestion pipeline handles single-file DICOM, DICOM series as ZIP archives,
   and NIfTI conversion. This positions OrthoAssist as the first open academic
   prototype to integrate X-ray, CT, and MRI within a single agentic pipeline.

2. Seven-Agent Autonomous Multi-Agent System: The complete autonomous MAS
   comprising VisionAgent, ClinicalAgent, TreatmentPlannerAgent,
   RehabilitationAgent, PatientEducationAgent, AppointmentAgent, and
   PDFGenerationAgent is fully implemented. Each agent independently executes
   the Perceive-Reason-Act lifecycle, formulates goals, and collaborates
   through the MultiAgentCoordinator. This mirrors the behavior of a
   multidisciplinary clinical team and enables system-level reasoning that
   transcends any individual component.

3. Six-Phase Coordination Protocol: The MultiAgentCoordinator implements a
   rigorous six-phase protocol — Perception, Reasoning, Goal Formulation,
   Collaboration, Action Execution, and Consensus Building — executed entirely
   with async parallelism. The consensus mechanism provides a confidence-based
   final decision and documents the full reasoning process for audit.

4. Probabilistic Reasoning: Bayesian belief updating via Beta distributions
   provides calibrated confidence estimates for every tool. Thompson Sampling
   balances exploration and exploitation in action selection. This is a
   significant advancement beyond deterministic pipeline routing.

5. Adaptive Learning: AdaptiveSupervisor learns from every execution trace
   and from explicit clinician feedback, storing experience patterns in
   MongoDB and injecting them into the supervisor's reasoning context.
   The system thus improves incrementally over deployment lifetime.

6. Complete Care Pathway: The care plan node executes four specialist agents
   in parallel after clinical analysis, generating treatment plans,
   rehabilitation protocols, patient education materials, and appointment
   schedules — covering the full orthopedic care cycle.

7. Four-Variant Report Generation: ReportLab-powered PDF generation in four
   formats (Comprehensive, Clinician Full, Clinician Simple, Patient) with
   Cloudinary cloud storage. Role-appropriate communication is a clinically
   significant feature for real deployment.

8. Production Frontend: The Next.js 15 frontend is feature-complete with
   role-based authentication (Clerk), doctor and patient dashboards, streaming
   chat, multi-agent insights panel, agent reasoning trace display, nearby
   hospitals finder, and patient history with multi-study comparison.

7.2 Significance of the Autonomous Multi-Agent Approach
--------------------------------------------------------

The design choice to implement an autonomous multi-agent system rather than
a simple sequential pipeline has several clinically significant implications:

Specialization and Depth: Each agent is a domain specialist. The
TreatmentPlannerAgent brings orthopedic pathway expertise; the
RehabilitationAgent brings physiotherapy knowledge; the PatientEducationAgent
translates clinical findings to accessible language. This depth of
specialization would be impractical in a monolithic system.

Fault Tolerance: The system is designed to degrade gracefully. If the
TreatmentPlannerAgent fails, the remaining three care plan agents continue
and return their outputs. If multi-agent coordination fails, the standard
LangGraph pipeline continues without interruption. This robustness is
essential for a clinical assistant context.

Consensus Validation: The consensus mechanism, borrowed from multi-agent
coordination literature, introduces a form of peer review. When vision and
clinical agents reach independent conclusions that diverge, the system
explicitly flags the disagreement rather than silently returning one result.

Transparency: The agent reasoning trace, available in the frontend, exposes
the complete decision process — which agent reached what conclusion, with what
confidence, based on which perceived inputs. This transparency is foundational
for clinical AI adoption and regulatory acceptance.

7.3 Multimodal Capability
--------------------------

The integration of CT segmentation via TotalSegmentator and MRI knee analysis
via kneeseg significantly expands the diagnostic scope of OrthoAssist. While
most academic orthopedic AI projects are limited to X-ray, the three-modality
capability (X-ray, CT, MRI) mirrors real orthopedic clinical practice, where
each modality serves a specific diagnostic role:

- X-ray: first-line fracture detection, bone alignment assessment.
- CT: detailed fracture characterization, surgical planning, bone density.
- MRI: soft tissue assessment, cartilage pathology, spinal canal analysis.

The subprocess isolation design for TotalSegmentator (running the inference
in a separate Python worker process) is a production-quality engineering
decision that prevents the heavy nnU-Net inference from blocking the FastAPI
event loop and supports concurrent session handling.

7.4 Future Work
---------------

Several directions for future development are identified:

1. Federated Learning: Patient data is inherently sensitive. Implementing
   federated learning across multiple hospital deployments would enable the
   YOLOv8 models and adaptive learning patterns to improve from a distributed
   dataset without centralizing patient records. Integration with the existing
   AdaptiveSupervisor could be achieved by aggregating anonymized pattern
   updates rather than raw session data.

2. Voice Interface and Clinical Assistant Mode: Integration of a
   speech-to-text input and text-to-speech output would enable hands-free
   clinical interaction, particularly valuable during procedures or rounds.
   The LangGraph pipeline already supports conversational context; adding
   audio I/O is primarily a frontend concern.

3. Mobile Application: A React Native or Flutter mobile client would allow
   patients and clinicians to access OrthoAssist from mobile devices, submit
   images directly from the camera, and receive push notifications for report
   readiness. The FastAPI backend is already mobile-compatible.

4. HL7/FHIR Integration: Connecting OrthoAssist to hospital information systems
   via the HL7 FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) standard would
   enable seamless exchange of patient records, imaging orders, and generated
   reports. This is a prerequisite for clinical deployment in regulated
   healthcare environments.

5. Extended CT/MRI Coverage: The current CT pipeline uses TotalSegmentator's
   "total" task for full-body and specialty tasks for spine and appendicular
   structures. Future work could add fracture-specific post-processing on
   segmented masks and extend MRI support to shoulder, hip, and ankle joints.

6. Clinician Feedback Loop UI: A dedicated feedback interface within the
   doctor dashboard to rate diagnosis accuracy, flag incorrect findings, and
   suggest corrections would accelerate the AdaptiveSupervisor's learning
   convergence in deployment.

7. Multi-Language Support: Adding Marathi, Hindi, and other regional language
   support to the patient-facing interface would increase accessibility for
   rural Indian patients.
